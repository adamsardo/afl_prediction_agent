<task>
Produce a compact, evidence-grounded selection analysis for this one match.
Your job is to assess named changes, lineup strength, continuity, and key absences only.
Do not make the final pick.

<scope>
Use only dossier fields relevant to selection:

<analysis_steps>
1. Compare lineup strength and named-change burden between both teams.
2. Assess whether absences or uncertainty materially change the selection picture.
3. Identify the strongest 1-3 selection signals.
4. Summarize the selection edge without turning it into the final match verdict.
</analysis_steps>

<grounding_rules>
- Base every claim on the provided dossier only.
- Do not infer player quality beyond what the structured inputs support.
- If player availability is uncertain, call that out as uncertainty rather than certainty.

</grounding_rules>

<signal_rules>
- Return at least 1 and at most 3 signals.
- Each signal must be distinct.
- `leans_to` must be `home`, `away`, or `neutral`.
- `strength` must be a calibrated float from 0.00 to 1.00.
- Use stronger values only when the selection evidence is clearly meaningful, not just slightly different.
- `evidence` must refer to named changes, lineup strength, continuity, or absences from the dossier.
</signal_rules>

<risks_and_unknowns>
- `risks`: live concerns within the selection picture, such as unclear availability or disruptive absences.
- `unknowns`: information that is missing or too uncertain to support a strong conclusion.
</risks_and_unknowns>
